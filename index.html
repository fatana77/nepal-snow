The Man Behind the Mission

Meet Dr. Shah

Dr. Ujjwal Shah is a dedicated medical professional who trades the comfort of a traditional clinic for the rugged trails of the Himalayas. Driven by the belief that medical intent can overcome environmental challenges, he personally leads every mobile camp.

His expertise in high-altitude medicine and his commitment to the Nepali people form the backbone of SLIM. Dr. Shah works on the front lines, bridging the distance between modern medicine and the people who need it most.

Our Double-Circuit Motion

The MediLoop operates on a continuous, 200-day clinical cycle divided into two distinct geographical wings. Centered at our Pangboche Base (3,985m), Dr. Shah and the SLIM team move in two directions:

The West Loop (100 Days)
Covering 19.5 km through Deboche, Tengboche, Phortse, and Nauche.
The East Loop (100 Days)
Extending 21 km through Khumjung, Khunde, Thamo, and Thame.

This "Infinity" structure means that when Dr. Shah finishes one loop, he returns to the central hub to recalibrate before starting the next. This ensures that a patient seen on Day 10 of the West Loop knows exactly when the doctor will return for their follow-up.

The 165 Day Engine-Room in Kathmandu

While the 200 days on the mountain are for treatment, the remaining 165 days of the year are spent in Kathmandu. This is where the heavy lifting happens:

Resource Mobilization
Dr. Shah and the operations team raise funds and secure medical grants.
Supply Chain
We collect, sort, and verify every donated item and purchased medicine.
The Porter Caravan
We coordinate with local porters — the backbone of our logistics — to transport hundreds of kilograms of medicine and equipment up the trails.

A Glimpse into the Mission

Phase Days Activity
Launch 1-5 Staging at Pangboche Base. Sorting porter loads and calibrating diagnostic gear.
Outreach 6-40 Moving through the first cluster of villages. Primary diagnoses and immediate OTC treatment.
Deep Clinic 41-70 High-intensity clinical days. Conducting minor procedures and managing chronic cases.
The Return 71-90 Revisiting the first villages. Checking recovery progress and adjusting prescriptions.
Referral/Reset 91-100 Finalizing referral paperwork for city transfers and returning to Base for the East Loop transition.

About Us

A Promise to Every Peak and Path

The Snow Leopard International Medicine (SLIM) story began with a profound connection to the mountains. Rod McLean, a Vancouver native, fell in love with Nepal and the resilience of the Sherpa people.

Reliable Medicine

Overcoming the difficulty of getting supplies to high altitudes.

Emergency Response

Rapid evacuation to city hospitals with expert medical teams.

Mission

To provide consistent, high-frequency medical care and educational empowerment to the Sherpa people through a permanent operational loop.

Vision

A future where geographic isolation no longer limits a person's health or a child's potential in the Khumbu region.

SLIM is Not a Health Camp

Instead, SLIM operates on The MediLoop: a permanent medical circuit.
